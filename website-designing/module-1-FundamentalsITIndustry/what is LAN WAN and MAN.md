# What is LAN WAN and MAN
LAN, MAN, and WAN are classifications of computer networks categorized strictly by their geographical size and scope. They dictate how devices, from single computers to global systems, connect and share resources.

1. LAN(Local Area Network)
A LAN connects computers and devices within a highly limited geographical area, such as a single home, office, or school building.
* Scope: 1 to 10 Kilometers.
* Speed: Extremely fast (often 1 Gbps to 100 Gbps).
* Examples: Your home Wi-Fi network, a university computer lab, or an office network where printers and files are shared internally.

2. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network)
A MAN is a larger network that spans across a city or a large campus. essenially bridging the gap between a LAN and a WAN.
* Scope: 5 to 10 kilometers.
* Speed: Generally moderate to fast.
* Example: A cable television network spanning a city, or a city-wide government network connecting all municipal buildings and librarries.

3. WAN (Wide Area Network)
A WAN spans vast geographical distances, connecting multiple smaller networks (like LANs) across countries, continents, or the entire globe.
* Scope: Spans thousands of kilometers.
* Speed: Generally slower than LANs due to long distances, signal conversions, and congestion.
* Example: The ultimate WAN is the Internet itself. it is also utilized by multinational corporations to connect branch offices across the world.
